<td>Yosemite features sheer granite cliffs, exceptionally tall waterfalls, and old-growth forests at a unique intersection of geology and hydrology. <a href="/wiki/Half_Dome" title="Half Dome">Half Dome</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Capitan" title="El Capitan">El Capitan</a> rise from the park's centerpiece, the glacier-carved <a href="/wiki/Yosemite_Valley" title="Yosemite Valley">Yosemite Valley</a>, and from its vertical walls drop <a href="/wiki/Yosemite_Falls" title="Yosemite Falls">Yosemite Falls</a>, one of North America's tallest waterfalls at 2,425 feet (739&#160;m) high. Three giant sequoia groves, along with a pristine wilderness in the heart of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_(U.S.)" title="Sierra Nevada (U.S.)">Sierra Nevada</a>, are home to a wide variety of rare plant and animal species.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> (WHS)<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></td>

<td>Located at the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Plateau" title="Colorado Plateau">Colorado Plateau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Basin" title="Great Basin">Great Basin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a>, this park contains sandstone features such as mesas, rock towers, and canyons, including the <a href="/wiki/The_Narrows_(Zion_National_Park)" title="The Narrows (Zion National Park)">Virgin River Narrows</a>. The various sandstone formations and the <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">forks</a> of the Virgin River create a wilderness divided into four ecosystems: <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riparian" class="mw-redirect" title="Riparian">riparian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coniferous_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Coniferous forest">coniferous forest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup></td>
